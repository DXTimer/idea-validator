# The riskiest assumption

**The leap of faith:** there exists a revenue-on-the-line solo professional for whom
cross-channel **"never-drop" detection** (catching the high-stakes follow-up that's
about to slip) is an unsolved, payable painkiller — one their existing CRM or
sales-engagement tool does not already handle.

Why it's the one that matters: the [what-has-to-be-true table](what-has-to-be-true.md)
shows the as-framed idea already failed on autonomy (#1) and incumbent bundling (#2).
The only way the idea lives is the reframe, and the reframe stands or falls on this one
condition. If the value is really *drafting* (not detection), or if CRMs already nag
them, there is no wedge. Everything downstream depends on it.

The Adversary sharpened this: high-stakes messages are exactly the ones people insist
on writing themselves, so a draft tool adds review work without removing the part they
care about. The valuable, payable unit has to be the **catch**, and the catch has to be
something Bullhorn / HubSpot / Pipedrive / Mixmax don't already do.

# The cheapest way to find out

**Experiment:** a paid, 2-week **Wizard-of-Oz concierge**, done entirely by hand — which
is also the offer ("The Saved Placement Pilot", see [offer.md](offer.md)). Recruit
solo technical recruiters / boutique agency owners, get a $500 deposit, then manually
watch their email + calendar + LinkedIn + Slack and send a daily "about to slip" list.
On day one, dig out every already-rotting thread from the last 30 days.

Run it as a paid pre-sale because money is the only customer-development answer that
can't be polite. A deposit proves three things at once: the pain is felt as lost money
(not annoyance), they'll pay for detection (not drafting), and the wedge beats "my CRM
already does this."

**Cost / time:** under $200 and ~2 weeks of hand-running 5 pilots. No product built.

**Success criterion:** of 50 qualified solo recruiters pitched, **7 or more pay the
$500 deposit** within two weeks (≥14% on warm outreach); they let you draft rather than
rewriting from scratch; and they currently track follow-ups in something ad hoc, not a
CRM that already reminds them.

**Kill criterion:** fewer than 7 of 50 pay; OR they dropped follow-ups because they had
no time to *write* (drafting is the job — commodity); OR their CRM already nags them; OR
they shrug at a caught follow-up. **Second-order kill:** of those who pay for the
concierge, if fewer than half opt into software at launch, you built a service, not a
product.

A fake-door landing page alone would mislead — it measures inflated stated intent for an
unbuilt product and never separates drafting from detection. Use the
[landing page](artifacts/landing-page-prompt.md) only as a secondary demand signal; the
paid pilot is the decision.
