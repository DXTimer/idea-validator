# Customer interview script — solo technical recruiters

Mom-Test rules: ask about their **past behavior and real money**, never pitch, never
ask "would you use." If you catch yourself describing the product, stop. The two probes
in **bold** are the ones that separate the real risk (detection) from the comfortable
answer (drafting) — do not skip them.

Aim for 15-20 of these before reading the deposit numbers as signal. 15 minutes each.

## Warm-up (their world)
1. "Walk me through yesterday. Where did your time actually go?"
2. "How many live candidates and open reqs are you juggling right now?"
3. "Where does all of it live — Gmail, LinkedIn, a CRM, your head?"

## The pain (past tense, real stakes)
4. "Tell me about the last deal or candidate you lost. What happened?"
5. **"The last time a follow-up slipped, was it because you forgot it existed, or because you knew but didn't have time to write it?"** (forgot → detection is the job; no-time → drafting is the job, which is commodity)
6. "How often does something important go quiet on you? Give me the last specific time."
7. "What did that cost you — roughly, in fee terms?"

## Current solution & spend (follow the money)
8. **"Show me how you track follow-ups today."** (watch them — if their CRM already nags them, the wedge is weaker; if it's sticky notes and memory, it's stronger)
9. "What do you pay for today to keep your pipeline from leaking — CRM, sourcing tools, a VA, anything?"
10. "What does that cost a month? What made you start paying for it?"
11. "Last time you hired a human to help with this, what happened?"

## The gap (let them say it)
12. "If you could wave a wand and fix one thing about how you keep deals from slipping, what would it be?"
13. "What have you already tried for that, and why did you stop?"

## Soft close (commitment signal, not a pitch)
14. "I'm putting together a small thing where someone watches your channels for two weeks and flags the follow-ups about to slip, money-back if it doesn't catch one. Who do you know who needs that most?" (referral ask doubles as a true-interest test — do they volunteer themselves?)
15. If they lean in: "Want me to send you the details? It's $500 for the two weeks, catch-one-or-free." (note whether interest survives contact with a price)

## What you're listening for
- Dropped follow-ups described as **lost money**, not annoyance → painkiller.
- They **forgot** more than they **ran out of time to write** → detection is the wedge.
- Current tracking is **ad hoc**, not a CRM that already reminds them → room to win.
- Interest **survives the price** → real willingness to pay.

If most interviews come back the other way (annoyance, no-time-to-write, CRM already
nags, interest dies at price), that's the kill signal — stop, don't build.
