# The Panel

The load-bearing finding from each specialist. Full market and competitor sourcing at
the bottom.

## Market — Priya
Budget is real but scattered (Superhuman $30, Motion $19-29, Reclaim $8-18, human VAs $500-5,000/mo). SAM ~$0.7-1B where top-down (AI assistant market $3.35B in 2025, 44.5% CAGR) and bottoms-up (~1.5M ambitious solopreneurs × ~$480/yr ≈ $720M) converge — a good sign the number is real. Good-business, borderline venture-scale. Biggest risk: the high-willingness-to-pay slice is thin (most of the headline 29.8M solopreneurs earn under $25K), and incumbents who own the inbox can collapse the standalone budget.

## Customer — solo pre-PMF founder
Pull strength **4/10**. "My problem isn't volume I need automated, it's judgment and stakes. The handful of things that matter are exactly what I'll never let software do unsupervised, and the stuff it could safely run is the stuff I already ignore for free." Would pay $15-25/mo only after it earned trust as draft-and-surface; would cancel Superhuman before the database. Recoils at acting in their name: "one wrong send to an investor is irreversible and the upside is saving me 15 minutes."

## Competitor — Marcus
Moat **1/5**. Crowded and funded — Cora ($15/mo, "chief of staff in your inbox"), Fyxer ($17M ARR, 180k users, ~$40M raised), Lindy (400k pros, ~$50M raised, $49.99/mo), Martin, alfred_, Blockit (Sequoia seed). And shipping as a platform feature *now*: Gemini Spark Daily Brief, Microsoft Copilot Agent Mode for Outlook + Scout, ChatGPT Pulse, Superhuman Go. "Time to ship the equivalent: zero quarters. It is live." Only a thin cross-app (Gmail+Calendar+Slack) trust seam survives copying, and it shrinks as MCP-style connectors commoditize.

## Operator — Reggie
Executability **4/10**. The autonomous core is un-shippable (cost of a wrong send), forcing a retreat to draft-and-approve; daily full-inbox inference runs $30-150/mo against a $30-50 price; Google's restricted-scope OAuth forces an annual CASA security audit that gates write access; the indie audience signs up cheap and churns. Only gestures at working if CAC is near-zero (founder's own audience) AND price pushes to ~$99 AND you cache aggressively to a cheap model — and two of those three fight each other.

## Pre-mortem — The Coroner
#1 cause of death: the **supervision tax**. Every comm is high-stakes, so the founder reviews everything the agent does, and the proactive notifications become a second inbox. Net time saved goes flat or negative, and the founder concludes it's faster to do it themselves. Early warning sign (month 2-3): draft edit/approval rate stays above ~50%, users keep it pinned to "suggest, don't send," and daily-brief open rates collapse after week 2. Category killer: **trust asymmetry** — the downside of a wrong autonomous action is catastrophic and irreversible while the upside is minutes, so users clamp autonomy to suggestions-only, deleting the exact wedge that was the differentiator.

## Where the panel disagreed with itself
- **Sloan vs Marcus on the moat.** Sloan: compounding personal context is the moat. Marcus: moat is 1/5 and the platforms own the same data first-party. The Customer settles it — they won't grant the access that builds the context.
- **Priya vs the Customer on the money.** Priya: the budget is real, ~$1B. Customer/Dutch: the buyer as framed is the brokest segment in software. Both right: the money exists, but not where this proposal points it.
