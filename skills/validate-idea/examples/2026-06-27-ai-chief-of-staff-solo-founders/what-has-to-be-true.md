# What Has To Be True

This is the bridge from the [dialectic](dialectic.md) to the decision. Sloan's bull
case is a stack of conditions that must hold; Dutch's fatal objections are conditions
that must not happen. Merged and ranked most-shaky first, here is everything that must
be true for this idea to win.

| # | Must be true | Status | Evidence / who flagged it | If false |
|---|--------------|:------:|---------------------------|----------|
| 1 | The target buyer grants an AI autonomous action over their highest-stakes comms (or the de-fanged "suggest only" version retains anyway) | **hoped → evidence says NO** | Customer pull 4/10, "scariest thing you could ask me to do"; Dutch's trust cliff | The only differentiator collapses into a commodity copilot |
| 2 | The core feature isn't bundled free by incumbents before you lock customers in | **hoped → already FALSE** | Marcus: Gemini Spark Daily Brief, Copilot for Outlook, ChatGPT Pulse are live now | No standalone budget, no reason to install a third-party agent |
| 3 | A reachable buyer exists who pays $50-150/mo and isn't already satisfied by free AI or their CRM | **believed (shaky)** | Priya: ~$1B SAM but the high-WTP slice is thin; reframe bets on this buyer | No business |
| 4 | The payable unit is *detection* (catching the slip), and detection is hard enough that CRMs/sequencers don't already solve it | **hoped** | The Adversary: high-stakes messages are exactly what people write themselves; CRMs already nag | You're selling review work nobody wants; commodity |
| 5 | Unit economics survive — you can monitor a full inbox daily for less than the price | **believed (shaky)** | Reggie: inference $30-150/mo can equal or exceed a $30-50 price | Negative gross margin at the only price the segment tolerates |
| 6 | There's a cheap, repeatable channel to the buyer that actually retains | **believed (shaky)** | Reggie: indie audience reachable but cheap and churny | CAC outruns LTV; growth stalls |
| 7 | The market is big enough to be worth it | **known-ish** | Priya: SAM ~$0.7-1B, top-down and bottoms-up converge | Good small business at best, not venture-scale |

**Status legend:** known (evidence says yes) · believed (reasonable, untested) ·
hoped (load-bearing, no evidence).

## The verdict that falls out of this table

Conditions #1 and #2 are already failed for the idea **as framed** (autonomous chief of
staff for broke solo founders). That's why the literal pitch is a PASS — two
independent load-bearing conditions are false, not merely uncertain.

The idea only survives by abandoning the conditions that failed and re-betting on #3
and #4 with a different buyer. So for the go-forward (reframed) decision, **the shakiest
live link is #4: that detection is the payable unit and is genuinely unsolved by the
buyer's existing CRM.** That is the riskiest assumption.
