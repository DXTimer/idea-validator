# Red Team — The Adversary

The Adversary saw only the verdict, the scores, the what-has-to-be-true list, the
riskiest assumption, and the test — not the panel's reasoning. It challenged in both
directions, and the verdict moved.

## What it found

**Too kind:** the reframe was sold as escaping the differentiation cap, but it imports
the same zero-moat problem. For revenue-on-the-line client-service pros, the incumbent
is no longer Gemini/Copilot/Pulse — it's CRM and sales-engagement tooling (HubSpot,
Pipedrive, Streak, Mixmax, Apollo, Bullhorn for recruiters) that already does
cross-channel follow-up sequencing and reminders. Differentiation stays 1/5 after the
reframe, so the reframe does not lift the cap it claimed to. Market 4/5 was also
generous once narrowed to one niche, and risk-testability 4/5 over-credited a fake-door.

**Too harsh:** killing autonomy outright. Draft-and-approve is the one capability every
incumbent already ships — it is the commodity. Autonomy on *low-stakes* categories
(scheduling, triage, routine chasing) is the only uncommoditized surface. Declaring the
autonomy assumption "already failed" with no test threw away the sole candidate
differentiator.

**Riskiest assumption check (disagree):** the real riskiest assumption is that the
bottleneck is drafting, not remembering. The wedge is literally "never drop" =
detection, but high-stakes follow-ups are exactly the messages people insist on writing
themselves, so draft-and-approve adds review work without removing the part they care
about, while detection is already in every CRM. The real bet: the payable unit is AI
*drafting* rather than a reminder, and "never drop" detection is hard enough that
CRMs/sequencers don't already solve it.

**Biggest blind spot:** the CRM / sales-engagement incumbent set (the verdict named
only consumer AI). Secondary misses: Google CASA security-review cost and latency for a
solo founder needing write access, and approve-fatigue as a churn failure mode.

**Does the test test the risk:** no, partly. The fake-door pre-sale tests inflated
stated WTP, not behavior, and never separates drafting from detection. Replace or pair
it with a 2-week Wizard-of-Oz concierge: manually surface 5-10 operators' dropped
follow-ups and observe whether they valued the catch and whether they let you draft or
rewrote it themselves.

## What changed as a result

- **Confidence cut from medium to low.**
- **Market 4 → 3** and **risk-testability 4 → 3** (total 26 → **24/50**).
- The riskiest assumption was **re-aimed from drafting/autonomy to detection-vs-drafting**
  (now condition #4 in [what-has-to-be-true.md](what-has-to-be-true.md)).
- The incumbent to beat was corrected to **CRMs, not Gemini**, in the reframe.
- The test was **swapped from a fake-door to a paid concierge** that separates the real
  bet from the comfortable one — and the interview script now probes "forgot vs no time
  to write" and "show me how you track follow-ups today."

Net (The Adversary's call): keep the direction, drop the confidence, re-aim the wedge at
detection over drafting, name CRMs as the incumbent, and run a concierge test — the
fake-door alone would have handed a false green light. Adopted in full.
