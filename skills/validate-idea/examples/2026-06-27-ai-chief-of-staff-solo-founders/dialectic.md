# The Dialectic — Sloan vs Dutch

Same idea, opposite jobs, neither saw the other.

## Sloan, the believer (7/10 it's a real opportunity)

- **The 10x version:** it becomes the default operating layer for one-person companies, the thing a solo founder switches on day one, like Stripe for payments. Over time it runs scheduling, follow-up chasing, CRM hygiene, and light vendor coordination, replacing a ~$3k/mo offshore EA with a $30/mo agent.
- **Who desperately needs it:** the solo founder running their own sales and fundraising, where a dropped follow-up is directly lost money. Superhuman makes you faster but you still do the work; Calendly only books; a human VA costs $35k+/yr and stumbles on context-heavy threads. None of them notice "the investor who replied 6 days ago is going cold" and act.
- **Why now:** inference to read a full inbox fell from ~$30/1M tokens (GPT-4, early 2023) to a fraction of a cent (Gemini Flash, GPT-4o-mini, Haiku); reliable function-calling plus MCP standardized acting on Gmail/Calendar/Slack; ~800M weekly ChatGPT users already trust AI with real work.
- **The wedge:** inbox triage + a proactive daily brief (read-mostly, low blast radius), then graduate to scheduling, then autonomous follow-up chasing, then draft-and-send inside learned guardrails. Each step is a small permission upgrade earned by demonstrated reliability.
- **Possible moat:** personal-context data that compounds (who matters to you, your voice, your priorities), building Superhuman-style switching cost; the taste to know when to act vs ask; and Google's restricted-scope security assessment as a cost-of-entry that keeps weekend clones out.
- **Strongest reason to bet:** the low-risk wedge is independently useful and monetizable on day one, yet every day of use builds a personal dataset that makes the agent stickier and more capable of acting autonomously.

## Dutch, the skeptic (8/10 it fails as framed)

- **Most likely failure:** the one differentiator (acting on its own judgment) is a trust cliff, not a feature. The first auto-sent tone-deaf reply to an investor, double-booked customer call, or wrong cancellation ends trust permanently, because a founder's inbox is where deals, hiring, and fundraising live. Users downgrade it to "ask me first," at which point it's a copilot competing with free Gmail Gemini and $30/mo Superhuman.
- **Why no switch/pay:** Gmail is free and good-enough; "letting things slip" is how founders already triage. Pre-PMF founders guard every dollar, and the budget holder is their own credit card, the most price-sensitive buyer alive. The moment they can afford help, they hire a real VA with accountability.
- **Timing risk:** wrong in both directions. Too early for the autonomy that's the whole pitch (LLMs still hallucinate enough that unsupervised high-stakes sends are reckless), and too late as a standalone (the category is in every recent YC batch).
- **The graveyard:** x.ai (Amy/Andrew) raised $44M, leaned on humans, killed the product in 2021. Clara Labs and Julie Desk stalled and got absorbed. Fin pivoted to analytics then sold. Zirtual laid off 400 overnight in 2015 when the economics flipped. Google Inbox built proactive triage and Google killed it in 2019, folding the good parts into Gmail. Pattern: either the AI isn't reliable so you pay humans, or the platform absorbs the feature.
- **Feature-not-a-company:** yes. Google ships it as Gemini in Workspace, Microsoft as Copilot in Outlook/Teams, and Superhuman already does AI triage, auto-drafts, and summaries. The whole thing is an orchestration layer over APIs the platform owners control and can gate.
- **Fatal flaw:** the autonomy that's the only real moat is exactly what founders can't afford to trust in their highest-stakes channel, so they'll cripple it into a copilot the incumbents give away.

## Where they actually clash

**The autonomy.** Sloan's entire moat depends on the agent acting on its own and compounding context from doing so. Dutch says users will never grant that in their highest-stakes channel.

## Who's right, and why

Dutch, and the Customer breaks the tie. The actual buyer (see [panel.md](panel.md)) scores pull 4/10 and says the access that would build Sloan's compounding-context moat is "the single scariest thing you could ask me to do." The moat never gets the fuel it needs to compound. Sloan's why-now is real; Sloan's moat is not.
