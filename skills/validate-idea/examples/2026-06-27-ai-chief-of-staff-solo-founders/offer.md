# The Offer — designed by The Closer

The verdict's go-forward direction is the reframe: detection, not autonomy, sold to a
revenue-on-the-line buyer. The offer below is built for that buyer, and it *is* the
[validation test](riskiest-assumption-and-test.md). Principles it draws on:
[offer-playbook.md](../../references/offer-playbook.md).

**Avatar:** the solo contingency tech recruiter or 1-3 person boutique staffing-agency
owner billing $150k-$400k/year, running 15-30 live candidates and 6-12 client reqs
alone, pipeline scattered across Gmail, Google Calendar, LinkedIn DMs/InMail, and a
client Slack or two. Average placement fee $18k-$30k. They've personally lost a deal in
the last 90 days because a follow-up slipped, and it still stings.

**Dream outcome (quantified):** "Never let a $25k placement die in my inbox again."
Recover 1-2 placements a quarter that would have leaked = +$25k-$50k they were already
leaving on the table, with zero new tools to learn.

## The offer: "The Saved Placement Pilot"

For 14 days I personally watch your email, calendar, LinkedIn, and Slack and send you a
once-a-day "About to Slip" list: the 3-7 highest-stakes follow-ups quietly dying right
now (finalist silent 48h+ post-interview, offer awaiting a yes, client req untouched
for 3 days, a call tomorrow with no confirm). Day one, I dig out every already-rotting
thread from your last 30 days. You pay $500, and if I don't catch a single follow-up
worth more than that, you pay nothing and keep everything. One saved deal pays for it
50x. Saying no is betting you won't drop a single $25k deal in the next two weeks — a
bet you've already lost before.

## Value stack
- **Core:** 14-day done-by-hand concierge monitoring of email + calendar + LinkedIn +
  Slack, with a daily "About to Slip" list and same-day alerts on anything urgent.
- **Bonus 1 — 20-minute White-Glove Setup** (kills "I don't have time to onboard
  another tool"): I connect everything on one call, read-only, you do nothing.
- **Bonus 2 — Day-1 Pipeline Triage** (kills "what about the deals already in
  motion?"): I audit your open threads and hand you every stalled follow-up from the
  last 30 days — found money in the first 24 hours.
- **Bonus 3 — End-of-Pilot Saved Deals Report** (kills "I'll never know if it
  worked"): every follow-up I caught, the commission at risk on each, and your personal
  "drop pattern" showing exactly where your desk leaks revenue.

## Risk reversal / guarantee
**Catch one or it's free.** If by day 14 I haven't surfaced at least one follow-up
you'd otherwise have dropped worth more than the $500, I refund every dollar and you
keep the Saved Deals Report and the Pipeline Triage. NDA signed before access,
read-only, revocable any second. Your downside is a 20-minute call.

## Scarcity & urgency
I run this by hand, so I can hold **only 5 active pilots at once** — a real ceiling, not
a marketing number. The **first 10 founders** also lock a **$49/mo founding rate for
life** (list will be $99-149) and turn their $500 into **$1,000 of launch credit**. The
door closes when the 10 founding seats fill or in 14 days, whichever comes first.

## Price & cash-flow play
$500 for the 14-day pilot, **100% upfront** — the cash funds the work and is itself the
demand proof. Deposit-for-credit: at launch the $500 becomes **$1,000 of credit** toward
year one, plus the **$49/mo founding rate for life** (vs $99-149 list), with an
annual-prepay option of $490/year (two months free). Upfront-only is deliberate: a card
on file is the signal; "I'll pay when it's built" is not.

## Why it's irresistible
The value equation is maxed on every axis. Dream outcome is a $25k+ recovered placement;
perceived likelihood is near-certain because a real human does it and guarantees it in
writing; time delay collapses to 24 hours (found money from the Day-1 Triage); effort is
a 20-minute read-only setup. Big numerator, near-zero denominator, downside fully
reversed.

## How the offer validates the idea
A paid $500 deposit from an exact-avatar recruiter is the riskiest-assumption test in
one move. **Kill number:** pitch 50 qualified solo recruiters; if fewer than **7** pay
the $500 within 14 days (under 14% on warm outreach), the detection wedge is dead —
pivot. **Second-order signal:** of those who pay, if fewer than half opt into software
at launch, you built a one-time service, not a product.
